Lead Response Automation: The 47-Second Rule

Speed-to-lead is one of the simplest levers in sales. When the first response is slow, intent decays and your prospect moves on. When the first response is immediate, you stay in the conversation while the buyer still cares.

Lead Automation 6 min read | USA & UK
By Buzzwise Editorial • April 21, 2026

Why response time decides who wins

Most businesses think lead generation is the hard part. In reality, the hard part is what happens right after a lead comes in. If your first response lands hours later, you're not competing on offer, you are competing on attention, and you have already lost it.

The “47-second rule” is a simple idea, respond while the prospect is still in buying mode. That means replying in seconds, not later today, not tomorrow morning, and definitely not after the weekend.

What automation changes

Lead response automation makes your first response consistent. It ensures every inbound form, missed call, DMs, or ad click triggers an immediate acknowledgement, an intelligent next step, and a clean handoff to your team.

Instead of relying on someone to check a CRM queue, open an inbox, or remember to follow up, the system handles the first minutes automatically. Your sales team only steps in when the prospect is engaged, qualified, or ready to book.

The bottom line

If you are paying for leads, you are already paying for speed. Automation is how you stop wasting that spend. You respond instantly, follow up reliably, and you turn “we will get back to you” into booked calls.
